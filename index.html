Misconceptions and Evolution

Misconceptions

My first coding experience dates back to 2014, my senior year of high school. I started playing around with Tumblr's pre-built HTML and CSS themes. Looking at HTML and CSS syntax, I saw it as artistic. So I thought, I needed to show the world how artistic coding is. Henceforth, I became a computer science major at UNLV in 2014. I had my first introductory programming class in my sophomore year of college 2016. The class required us to code in C++, which was unfamiliar to me. Up until my senior year of high school to sophomore year of college, my idea of programming was only with HTML and CSS. It was certainly a humble beginnings.

Evolution

I never considered myself good with academics. I did well with assignments and projects but, did not excel with taking exams. Additionally, misconceptions and inexperience about programming did not help with my classes. However, that did not stop me from pursuing computer science. For two years, I pushed myself to work harder. Currently, my exam taking skills, solving problem skills, programming skills has all evolved. Curiosity has also led me this far to becoming a better programmer. Despite the failures and frustrations, I still see the beauty in all forms of programming. I hope to share them with others. Now that I'm in this position, I have reasons to be confident in my abilities.
